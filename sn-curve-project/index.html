Fatigue Strength

Fatigue strength is the maximum stress amplitude that a material can withstand for a specified number of cycles without failure. It's typically determined at 10⁶ to 10⁷ cycles.

Key Point: Fatigue strength decreases as the number of cycles increases.

Fatigue Life

Fatigue life is the number of stress cycles that a material can endure before failure occurs. It's the N value on the S-N curve for a given stress amplitude.

Key Point: Higher stress amplitudes result in shorter fatigue lives.

Endurance Limit

The endurance limit (or fatigue limit) is the stress amplitude below which a material can endure an infinite number of cycles without failure. Not all materials have a true endurance limit.

Key Point: Steel typically has an endurance limit, while aluminum does not.

S-N Curve Regions

  • Low Cycle Fatigue: < 10⁴ cycles, high stress
  • High Cycle Fatigue: 10⁴ - 10⁶ cycles
  • Very High Cycle Fatigue: > 10⁶ cycles

Factors Affecting Fatigue Life

Material Properties

Strength, ductility, microstructure, and surface finish

Loading Conditions

Stress amplitude, mean stress, frequency, and loading pattern

Environmental Factors

Temperature, corrosion, humidity, and chemical exposure

Geometric Factors

Stress concentrations, notches, surface roughness, and size effects
